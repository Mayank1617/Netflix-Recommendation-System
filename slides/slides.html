Problem Overview

  • Content discovery drives engagement on streaming platforms.
  • Data: Netflix Prize — 100M+ ratings, 480K users, 17.7K movies, 1–5★ with timestamps.
  • Goal: learn preferences → predict unseen ratings → generate useful ranked Top-K recommendations — not just minimise error.
  • Core challenge: a ~99% sparse user–item matrix.
We evaluate on both rating accuracy (RMSE) and ranking quality (MAP@10), and study the trade-off between them.

Data & Key Insights (EDA)

  • Ratings positively skewed (mean ≈ 3.6; 58% are 4–5★).
  • Ratings/user: mean 267 (50–2,981); ratings/movie: mean 3,566.
  • Popularity bias: top 20% of movies = 56% of all ratings.
  • Heavy long tail in both users and items.

Implication

Dense signal favours latent-factor models; the long tail demands attention to coverage for genuine discovery.

Approach

Dense, reproducible subset

Top 3,000 movies × 40,000 active users → 10.7M ratings @ 8.92% density. Temporal 80/20 split (predict each user's most recent ratings). Relevance ≥ 3.5.

Four models compared

  • Bias baseline — μ + user/item biases (RMSE floor)
  • Item-based CF — adjusted-cosine similarity (powers explanations)
  • Matrix Factorization (SVD) — 64 latent factors, SGD on GPU
  • Neural CF — MLP over embeddings (innovation)

Evaluation Methodology

  • RMSE — rating-prediction accuracy on held-out test ratings.
  • MAP@10 — for each user: score all movies → mask seen movies → take Top-10 → Average Precision vs relevant (≥3.5) test movies.
  • Also: Precision@10, Recall@10, NDCG@10, and catalog Coverage.
  • Temporal split avoids the leakage of a random split — realistic, deployment-like evaluation.

Key Insights & Future Work

Insights

  • On dense data, latent factors (SVD) give the best accuracy–ranking blend.
  • Always report ranking metrics, not just RMSE — they disagree.
  • Popularity bias is measurable (coverage) and worth optimising for discovery.

Future work

  • Cold start: popularity fallback, onboarding ratings, content-based hybrid.
  • Ranking-aware training (BPR/WARP); SVD++ & temporal dynamics.
  • Coverage/diversity re-ranking to counter popularity bias.
